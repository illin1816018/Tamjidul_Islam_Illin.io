Research Works

Downscaling Climate Model Data Using Machine Learning and Assessing the Impact of Climate Change on the Rainfall of Potomac Basin

Undergraduate Thesis

In this study, my downscaled output was at 0.17x0.2 degrees, visualizing the impact of climate change on precipitation under SSP 3-7.00 scenarios.

Thesis Supervisor: Dr. A.T.M. Hasan Zobeyer

Riparian Management and Riverbank Erosion: A Case Study on Harirampur Upazila

Presented at 6th International Conference on Advances in Civil Engineering (ICACE-2022), CUET, Bangladesh

Our paper presents a case study on managing riverbank erosion through riparian management along the Padma River in Harirampur Upazila. Using GIS and NDVI analyses, we propose eco-friendly guidelines for sustainable riverbank protection.

Supervisor: Mushtari Maliha

Soil Erosion Assessment in Rangamati District: Comparing Hydrological Conditions & Geospatial Data Before & After the 2017 Landslide Event

Presented at 4th International Conference on Planning, Architecture & Civil Engineering (ICAPCE-2023), RUET, Bangladesh

This research examines the effects of land use and land cover changes on flash flood patterns in Sylhet, Bangladesh, highlighting increased urban development and reduced forest cover impacts on flood timing using the HEC-HMS model.

Prediction of Land Use Land Cover (LULC) Changes at Sirajganj in Northwest Region of Bangladesh with Machine Learning Algorithm (CA-ANN) and Evaluating Their Impact on Land Surface Temperature

Presented at American Geophysical Union (AGU) 2023

This study uses satellite imagery and machine learning models to predict LULC changes in Sirajganj, Bangladesh, and examines the impact of urban expansion and vegetation loss on land surface temperature.

Evaluating the Impact of LULC Changes on Flash Flood Using HEC-HMS: A Case Study for Sylhet Region

Presented at 7th International Conference on Civil Engineering for Sustainable Development (ICCESD 2024), KUET, Bangladesh

Our paper highlights how urban development and deforestation accelerate flash floods in Sylhet, Bangladesh, with flood lag times decreasing from 12 hours to 7 hours due to increased impervious surfaces.
